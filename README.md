Good Boy PvP Architecture: The "Mosh Pit" Arena
The Battlefield & Deployment
The PvP arena is a chaotic, lane-less "mosh pit" where players deploy their pack using a regenerating "Zoomie" energy meter (capping at 10). Instead of marching down rigid paths toward a base, deployed units immediately lock onto the nearest enemy and sprint into combat, kicking up a massive, cartoonish "dust ball." Players can drop heavy tanks or swarms of smaller units directly into these existing dust balls to swing the underlying math of the brawl. When a dust cloud resolves, the surviving dogs burst out and instantly rush the next closest target, creating a highly dynamic, snowballing battlefield.

Win Condition & Match Pacing
Matches are won through pure Ticket Attrition. Both players start with 15 tickets (lives). Every time a dog's HP is reduced to zero, it is comically launched out of the dust cloud, and its owner loses a ticket. The first player to lose 15 dogs loses the match. To enforce aggressive gameplay and prevent players from building ultra-defensive "turtle" decks just to stall, matches feature a strict time limit. If the clock runs out while the score is tied, the match ends in a hard draw where neither player receives any rewards whatsoever.

Economy & The Anti-Deranking System
Victorious players enter the "Dig Spot" gacha phase, where they are presented with six patches of dirt and get to dig up three to claim post-match loot (revealing the unpicked spots afterward to drive the "just one more game" loop). To organically combat intentional derankers, the loot pool is strictly tied to the player's current Arena Floor. High-level arenas offer exponentially better top-tier prizes (like Epic gear and massive gold payouts). Because of this drastic economic scaling, veteran players are forced to fight at their true skill ceiling, as intentionally dropping rank to beat up rookies would only yield worthless, low-level rewards.
